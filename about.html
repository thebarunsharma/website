🤹SkillShare

Skillshare is the Netflix of courses. They have courses on photography, graphic design, music production, freelancing, and more than 20,000 courses at very low montly price and you even get 2-months free if you sign up using links . And all the courses have hands-on project to try. I personally used it to learn graphic design, animation and web development. Skillshare was the best discovery during COVID and here are some of my favourites:

  1. The HTML CSS Course By Rich Armstrong
  2. Introduction to SEO for beginners
  3. Productivity Masterclass by Thomas Frank
  4. Advanced Class for Illustrator
  5. Creating Geometric animals using Illustrator

🚧Notion

It is the most flexible app I have ever used. It's mainly a note taking app but we can make Kanban style boards and calendars and also make *templates* . I use it to manage my life, studies. It can be also called as my *second brain.* I can switch from windows to Android and take all the college deadlines and assignments to information about running online courses.

🗃️Anki

It is the best , minimal flashcard program out there. It syncs seamlessly with my phone and computer. I used it first for learning Spanish language which I am at a basic level now. And I used it for taking notes of short answers of Microprocessor in Third Semester. It is too satisfying to finish all the cards for the day.
